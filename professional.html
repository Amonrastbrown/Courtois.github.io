Career

In 2011, Courtois signed with Chelsea FC in the English Premier League but was loaned immediately to Atlético Madrid in Spain. During his three seasons (2011–2014) with Atlético, he developed into one of the best goalkeepers in the world. He helped Atlético win the La Liga title (2013–14), the Copa del Rey (2013), and reach the UEFA Champions League Final (2014).

Courtois returned to Chelsea in 2014, becoming their first-choice goalkeeper. He won two Premier League titles (2014–15, 2016–17), the League Cup (2015), and the FA Cup (2018).

In 2018, Courtois transferred to Real Madrid, where he reached new heights. He won La Liga titles (2020, 2022), the Supercopa de España, and the 2022 UEFA Champions League, where he was named Man of the Match in the final against Liverpool. He also received the Yashin Trophy (2022) as the world's best goalkeeper.

Internationally, Courtois has been Belgium's starting goalkeeper since 2011, representing the country in multiple World Cups and Euros. He helped Belgium finish third at the 2018 FIFA World Cup, where he was awarded the Golden Glove for best goalkeeper.
